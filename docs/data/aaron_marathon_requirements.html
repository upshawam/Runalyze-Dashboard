Marathon shape

25.01.2026

The marathon shape is an invention of Runalyze to optimize prognoses for long distances, that are based on the effective VO2max. These prognoses normally can't consider whether the athlete has the optimal fitness or endurance for a marathon. The marathon shape is the solution for this problem, as the prognoses are continually adjusted for missing fitness.

Read more in our glossary

Effective VO2max
50,96 ml/kg/min
Optimal Marathon
3:07:39 (7:09/mi)

Your current effective VO2max is 50,96 which equals a marathon in 3:07:39. To realistically achieve this time, Runalyze expects you to complete an average weekly mileage of 54 mi and a weekly long run of 19 mi.

The points for your long runs are weighted by time and quadratic in distance. That means, a long jog yesterday gives more points than a long jog two weeks ago and a 30k-jog gives more points than two 20k-jogs.

In total, you have achieved 73% of your required weekly mileage and 69% of your required long runs. This results in a marathon shape of 71% which equals a marathon in 3:27:36.

Other distances

Our "Marathon Shape" model allows conversion to other distances. Please note once again: The "Marathon Shape" is not scientifically based and only serves as a rough estimate of whether you are sufficiently trained for a specific target distance (while the Effective VO2max only indicates the general performance level - independent of the distance/duration).
